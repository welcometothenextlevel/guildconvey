SA Property Law

What Makes SA Conveyancing Different

South Australia requires vendors to provide a Form 1 Vendor Statement before or at the time of signing the contract. This document discloses all relevant information about the property including title, encumbrances, planning, and rates.

SA buyers benefit from a 2-business-day cooling-off period after signing the contract, providing protection before they are legally bound to proceed.

Guild handles all SA-specific requirements including CITB levies, land tax clearances, and the Real Property Act searches unique to South Australia.
