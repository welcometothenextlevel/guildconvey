South Australia Property Law

What Makes South Australia Different

South Australia requires vendors to provide a Form 1 Vendor Statement before or at the time of signing the contract of sale. This discloses all material facts about the property, including title, encumbrances, planning, and rates. SA buyers benefit from a 2-business-day cooling-off period. Guild manages all SA-specific requirements including CITB levies, land tax clearances, and Real Property Act searches.

Common Questions

South Australia Conveyancing Questions

The Form 1 is a legal disclosure document vendors must provide in South Australia. It covers title details, encumbrances, council and water rates, planning information, and any other material facts about the property. Guild prepares Form 1s for sellers and reviews them for buyers — included in your fixed fee.

SA buyers have a 2-business-day cooling-off period after signing the contract. If you withdraw, you may be liable for the vendor's reasonable costs. Guild will advise you fully on your rights before you sign anything.

The Construction Industry Training Board levy applies to certain SA property transactions. Guild calculates and manages this as part of your conveyancing — included in your service, with the levy amount passed to you at cost.

No — a registered conveyancer can handle residential conveyancing in South Australia. Guild's SA team holds all required registrations and has deep expertise in SA property law.
