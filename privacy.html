Privacy Policy & Data Security

At Guild Conveyancing, protecting your privacy is not a legal formality — it is a core part of how we operate. This policy explains exactly what we collect, how we use it, and how we protect it.

1. Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Guild Conveyancing ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

We believe your privacy is a fundamental right, not a checkbox. Every system, process, and team member at Guild Conveyancing is held to a strict standard of data protection.

2. What Information We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Name, email address, and phone number (from quote and contact forms)
  • Property address and transaction details
  • Identity verification documents (required for conveyancing under Australian law)
  • Financial information relevant to your transaction

Information collected automatically

  • IP address and browser type (for security and analytics purposes only)
  • Pages visited and time spent on site (via anonymised analytics)
  • Device type (to optimise your experience)

We do not collect payment card details through this website. Any financial transactions are processed through secure, third-party platforms.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information solely to:

  • Provide you with conveyancing services
  • Respond to your quote requests and enquiries
  • Communicate about your property transaction
  • Comply with our legal obligations as a licensed conveyancing practice
  • Improve the security and performance of our website

We will never use your information for marketing without your explicit consent, and we will never sell your data to any third party — ever.

4. How We Protect Your Information

256-bit SSL Encryption

Every page of this website and every form submission is protected by 256-bit SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) encryption — the same standard used by Australian banks and financial institutions. This means all data transmitted between your browser and our servers is fully encrypted and cannot be intercepted.

Secure Data Storage

Your personal information and transaction documents are stored on access-controlled, encrypted servers. Only the team members directly assigned to your conveyancing file can access your information. No open databases. No unsecured file sharing.

PEXA Platform Security

Property settlement is conducted through PEXA (Property Exchange Australia) — a government-backed, independently audited electronic settlement platform with industry-leading security standards.

Internal Security Practices

  • All team members undergo privacy and security training
  • Access to client files is strictly role-limited and logged
  • We use multi-factor authentication on all internal systems
  • Regular security audits are conducted on our infrastructure

5. Disclosure of Your Information

We only share your personal information with:

  • Relevant legal and government authorities — as required by law for property transfers, land title registration, and revenue offices
  • Your mortgage lender — to coordinate settlement on your behalf
  • The other party's conveyancer or solicitor — as required to complete your transaction
  • PEXA — for electronic settlement processing

We do not share your information with advertisers, data brokers, marketing companies, or any unrelated third parties.

6. Your Rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information (subject to our legal obligations)
  • Withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au

7. Cookies

This website uses minimal, essential cookies only — to remember your session and improve your browsing experience. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels. You can disable cookies in your browser settings without affecting your ability to use this website.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal information and transaction records for 7 years following completion of your conveyancing matter, as required by Australian legal and professional standards. After this period, records are securely deleted.
