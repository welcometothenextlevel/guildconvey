Selling

A Section 32 (Victoria) or Form 1 (South Australia) is a legal document sellers must provide before a contract can be signed. It discloses title details, planning information, encumbrances, council rates, and owners corporation details. In Queensland, equivalent disclosures are contained in the REIQ contract. Guild prepares these documents as part of your selling service.

Sale proceeds are released electronically on settlement day via PEXA, typically within 1–2 hours of the scheduled settlement time. We notify you immediately once funds have been received.

State-Specific

In Victoria, buyers have 3 clear business days after signing to cool off, forfeiting 0.2% of the purchase price. Cooling-off does not apply at auction or within 3 business days either side of a publicly advertised auction.

Yes. Queensland law requires a qualified solicitor — a licensed conveyancer alone is not sufficient. All Guild QLD conveyancing is handled by our qualified legal practitioners. See our QLD page →

SA buyers have a 2-business-day cooling-off period after signing. If you withdraw, you may be liable for the vendor's reasonable costs. Guild will advise you on your rights before you sign anything.

QLD buyers have 5 business days from the contract date to cool off, forfeiting 0.25% of the purchase price. This right does not apply at auction or to contracts signed within 2 business days of an auction.
