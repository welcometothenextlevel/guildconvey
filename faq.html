Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you've been wondering about conveyancing in VIC, SA and QLD — answered clearly and honestly.

General

A fixed fee means you pay one flat price for the entire conveyancing service. There is no hourly billing, no ambiguity, and no surprise invoices at settlement. The only additional costs are government disbursements — title searches, council certificates, water rates — which we pass to you at cost with no mark-up whatsoever.

Never. Guild Conveyancing is built to operate 100% online. You sign documents electronically via secure e-signing platforms, verify your identity digitally, and communicate via email, phone, or video conference. Settlement itself is handled electronically through PEXA.

PEXA (Property Exchange Australia) is the digital platform used for over 99% of Australian property settlements. It replaces the old paper-based process where parties physically attended a settlement room with bank cheques. PEXA settles transactions electronically, typically completing within 1–2 hours on settlement day. Both your conveyancer and your lender must be PEXA registered — Guild is fully certified.

Timeframes depend on the settlement date agreed in your contract of sale. In Victoria, standard settlement is 30–60 days. In South Australia, 30 days is most common. In Queensland, 30–45 days. We begin work immediately upon engagement to ensure nothing delays your settlement date.

Selling

A Section 32 (Victoria) or Form 1 (South Australia) is a legal document sellers must provide before a contract can be signed. It discloses key information about the property — title details, council rates, planning information, encumbrances, and owners corporation details. In Queensland, equivalent disclosures are included in the REIQ contract itself. Guild prepares these documents as part of your selling service.

Sale proceeds are released electronically on settlement day via PEXA, typically within 1–2 hours of the scheduled settlement time. We notify you immediately once funds have been received.

State-Specific

In Victoria, buyers have 3 clear business days after signing a contract of sale to cool off. If you withdraw, you forfeit 0.2% of the purchase price. Cooling-off does not apply at auction or within 3 days either side of a publicly advertised auction.
