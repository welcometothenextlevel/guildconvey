Victoria Property Law

What Makes Victoria Different

Victoria requires vendors to provide a Section 32 Vendor Statement before a contract can be signed, disclosing title details, planning information, and encumbrances. Buyers have a 3-business-day cooling-off period after signing — and forfeit 0.2% if they withdraw. Standard settlement is 30–60 days, completed electronically via PEXA. Common Questions

Victoria Conveyancing Questions

A Section 32 (Vendor Statement) is a legal document sellers must provide before a contract is signed in Victoria. It discloses title details, planning overlays, council rates, owners corporation details, and any mortgages or encumbrances. Guild prepares these for sellers and reviews them thoroughly for buyers — included in your fixed fee.

Buyers have 3 clear business days after signing to cool off, forfeiting 0.2% of the purchase price. Cooling-off does not apply at auction or within 3 days either side of a publicly advertised auction.

Victorian first home buyers can access a full stamp duty exemption on properties up to $600,000, with a concession on properties up to $750,000. The $10,000 First Home Owner Grant is also available on new homes up to $750,000. Guild handles the application as part of your conveyancing service.

No — a licensed conveyancer is authorised to handle residential conveyancing in Victoria. Guild's VIC team are fully licensed and experienced. For complex matters, we engage qualified property solicitors.
